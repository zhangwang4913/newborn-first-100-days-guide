Section 05

Newborn Hospital Plan Guide

The 100-day window after birth is one of the most consequential financial decisions you will make. Here's why acting early protects your child for life.

CPF — MediSave Grant for Newborns
100
Days — The Critical Window
You have 100 days from birth to enrol your newborn in an Integrated Shield Plan without underwriting. Miss it and any documented conditions — however minor — may cause permanent exclusions or premium loadings for life.
Apply After 15 Days Old
Parents cannot enrol a newborn in an Integrated Shield Plan at birth. Once your child is past 15 days old, apply promptly to lock in their insurability — before any health conditions are documented in their medical records. Acting early gives your child the best chance of comprehensive, lifelong coverage.
Use This to Pay Premiums
$5,000
MediSave Grant for Newborns
Auto-credited to your newborn's CPF MediSave upon birth registration. Can be used directly to pay the annual IP main plan premium — no cash outlay needed.
Don't forget the Rider. An IP without a co-payment Rider still leaves you exposed to large deductibles and co-insurance. The most complete protection is to enrol both the main plan and a Rider within the 100-day window. Note: Rider premiums must be paid in cash and cannot be drawn from MediSave.

Section 08

4 Common Oversights New Parents Make

These are easy gaps to miss in the chaos of the first 100 days. Recognising them early is the simplest way to stay ahead.

1
Overinsuring the baby, underinsuring the breadwinner
Many parents pile into life insurance and savings policies on their newborn while leaving the family's primary income earner underprotected. If the breadwinner cannot work, no baby policy will replace that lost income. Always protect the engine that drives the family finances first.
2
Missing the 100-day health insurance window
This is one of the most time-sensitive decisions in personal finance. Miss it, and any health conditions documented in those first days — however minor — may be permanently excluded from your child's coverage for life. Remember: you cannot apply at birth — wait until the child is past 15 days old, then act promptly.
3
Leaving the CDA empty — forfeiting free government money
The CDA's dollar-for-dollar co-matching is a guaranteed 100% return. Yet many parents never deposit a single dollar, forfeiting up to $9,000 in free matching funds. Systematic CDA contributions are one of the highest-returning financial moves available to any Singaporean parent.
4
Not starting an education fund early enough
An overseas university degree — tuition, accommodation, and living costs included — runs around $200,000 today. At 3% annual cost inflation, that figure climbs well past $300,000 in 18 years. A family that starts a dedicated education fund at birth needs roughly $500 – $700 a month at 6% growth to reach that goal. A family that waits until the child starts primary school at age 7 needs roughly $1,000 – $1,400 a month — for the same outcome.
